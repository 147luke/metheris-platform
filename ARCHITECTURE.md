# Architecture

## Public Architecture Overview

This document provides a protected, non-operational overview of the Metheris platform architecture.

It is designed for professional evaluation and portfolio presentation only. It does not disclose the proprietary methods, internal workflows, implementation patterns, data structures, integration strategies or operational logic required to reproduce the system.

Metheris should be understood here as a conceptual multi-layer hydro-meteorological intelligence platform, not as an open-source architecture or public implementation blueprint.

---

## Architectural Positioning

Metheris is conceived to organize environmental complexity into structured territorial intelligence.

At a public level, its architecture can be described through five broad design orientations:

1. **Modular thinking**  
   The system is conceived as a set of separable conceptual domains, without exposing internal module boundaries or implementation rules.

2. **Multi-scale reasoning**  
   The platform is intended to reason across territorial scales, without disclosing proprietary scaling logic, geospatial models or aggregation methods.

3. **Observability-oriented design**  
   The system is framed around transparency, traceability and reliability, without publishing internal monitoring logic, metrics, thresholds or instrumentation details.

4. **AI-assisted interpretation**  
   AI is positioned as a support layer for interpretation and reporting, without exposing prompts, model choices, orchestration logic, training strategy or inference flows.

5. **Decision-support clarity**  
   The platform aims to transform complexity into readable outputs, without disclosing interface design, dashboard configuration, alerting logic or operational playbooks.

---

## Public Layer Model

The following layer model is intentionally abstract:

```text
[ Environmental Context ]
          ↓
[ Information Structuring ]
          ↓
[ Interpretation Layer ]
          ↓
[ Reliability Layer ]
          ↓
[ Decision-Support Layer ]
```

This representation is not a system specification. It is not sufficient to implement Metheris and must not be treated as a reusable architecture, template, framework or technical blueprint.

---

## 1. Environmental Context

Metheris is concerned with hydro-meteorological and territorial information.

At a public level, this may involve broad categories such as:

- meteorological observations
- hydrological indicators
- territorial signals
- historical environmental records
- geospatial context
- institutional or public-domain information sources

This repository does not identify proprietary source priorities, integration strategies, endpoint logic, collection methods, data-access policies or territorial intelligence criteria.

---

## 2. Information Structuring

The platform concept includes the transformation of fragmented information into coherent structures.

Publicly disclosed aspects are limited to general principles:

- organization of heterogeneous information
- conceptual normalization
- contextual enrichment
- preparation for interpretation
- preservation of traceability as a design value

This repository does not disclose ingestion workflows, validation rules, transformation logic, schemas, entity models, storage strategy, geospatial indexing or operational processing details.

---

## 3. Interpretation Layer

Metheris is designed to support interpretation of complex environmental situations.

At a high level, interpretation may involve:

- contextual reading of environmental signals
- identification of relevant changes
- support for reporting
- assistance in understanding complex scenarios
- decision-support summaries

This repository does not disclose anomaly models, predictive logic, risk scoring, classification rules, prompt chains, LLM workflows, model configurations, evaluation criteria or proprietary reasoning structures.

---

## 4. Reliability Layer

Reliability and observability are central to the Metheris vision.

At a public level, this means that the platform should be designed to support:

- visibility over system behavior
- reliability awareness
- traceability of information flows
- consistency checks
- accountability of outputs

This repository does not disclose monitoring architecture, metrics, alert thresholds, logging schemas, validation mechanisms, dashboard instrumentation, operational controls or incident-handling workflows.

---

## 5. Decision-Support Layer

The final goal of Metheris is to make complex environmental information readable for human users.

Publicly described outputs may include general interface concepts such as:

- territorial overviews
- environmental summaries
- monitoring views
- reporting views
- risk-awareness views
- decision-support representations

This repository does not disclose dashboard designs, UX flows, map logic, alert panels, user-role logic, reporting templates, operator consoles or commercial interface specifications.
